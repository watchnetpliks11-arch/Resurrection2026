Theme Background

Why This Message, Why Now

The ongoing conflict involving the US, Iran, Israel, the Middle East, and other nations taking sides is affecting the world economically and prompting churches to reexamine the fundamental reason why Jesus Himself built the church — and the character it embodies — so much so that not even the gates of Hades can overpower it (Matthew 16:18).

Central Questions We Are Asking
What is the connection between the Resurrection of Jesus and the present state of the world?
How do we celebrate this truth and manifest the presence and power of the kingdom, where Jesus is King, amid crisis, violence, and death?
What does it mean to live as new creations with new lives?
How does a glorious life look in a world where glamour and glory seem achievable through human means?

The Response to These Three Historical Events
NOW WE ALSO MAY LIVE NEW LIVES.

"If anyone is in Christ, he is a new creation; the old has gone, the new has come."

2 Corinthians 5:17

The new life Paul describes can only occur in Christ. But what does that look like if it is based on these three historical facts — Jesus' death, burial, and resurrection, witnessed by real people in history? How does this new life connect with Jesus' message: "Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is at hand" (Matthew 4:17)?

Theological Foundation

What New Life Truly Looks Like

Paul's Purpose in Romans

One reason the apostle Paul wrote the Epistle to the Romans was to deliberately proclaim the gospel in a place where diverse cultures existed and debates among highly intellectual and orderly people were common. Paul presented "baptism" not merely as a ritual but as a public declaration of two historical events witnessed by the world.

The Enemy's Strategy — And Why It Fails

Despite attempts by the enemy to erase these events through ideologies like atheism, communism, and liberalism, they remain the strongest evidence of the faith we hold and the message we continue to preach. They are the foundation for new lives.

The Restoration of Imago Dei

The resurrection of Jesus marks the restoration of the imago Dei — being created in the image of God — for all who believe in Him. Paul states in 1 Corinthians 4:20: "For the kingdom of God is not just a lot of talk; it is living by God's power." (NLT)

🌍
New Life in Action — A Living Testimony

This is why churches outside Iran, even in the face of radical Islam, have witnessed the remarkable growth of Christianity there. Iran today is home to one of the fastest-growing churches in the world. How can this be? This is what NEW LIFE looks like: not bowing to worldly kingdoms and moving according to the presence and power of the Holy Spirit boldly.
